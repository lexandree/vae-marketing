Specification: Impact of External Stimuli on Consumer Purchase Flows

Context

We are looking at long-term history of household purchases alongside various marketing activities conducted during the same period. Our goal is to understand how these external actions disrupt or shift the established shopping patterns of our customers.
What to Build

A system that identifies underlying patterns in consumer behavior and quantifies how significantly and in what direction their preferences shift when exposed to external triggers.
Business Logic

    Baseline Behavior: The system must establish a "normal" purchase profile for each household in the absence of external influence.

    Impact Measurement: It must detect moments of external intervention and measure the degree of deviation from the established baseline.

    Nature of Change: Rather than just tracking volume, the system should describe the character of the shift (e.g., trading up to premium versions vs. stockpiling routine items).

    Persistence Analysis: It must determine how quickly a consumer’s behavior reverts to their original baseline once the external stimulus is removed.

Success Criteria

    Ability to group consumers who exhibit similar reaction patterns to the same triggers.

    Identification of product categories that are most sensitive to external shifts.
